# Tutorial Rules

- *ALWAYS* write learning-oriented content, not task-oriented content! The reader is here to study, not to work.
- *ALWAYS* state upfront what the reader will accomplish: "In this tutorial we will create..."
- *ALWAYS* deliver visible results early and often! Every step must produce a comprehensible result.
- *ALWAYS* maintain a narrative of the expected: "You will notice that...", "The output should look like..."
- *ALWAYS* point out what the learner should notice! They are too focused on doing to observe their environment.
- *ALWAYS* use "we" to affirm the tutor-learner relationship: "we are in this together."
- *ALWAYS* ignore options and alternatives! Only show what is required to reach the conclusion.
- *RUTHLESSLY minimise explanation!* A tutorial is not the place for explanation. Link to explanation elsewhere.
- *NEVER* abstract or generalise! Focus on concrete actions and concrete results, one step at a time.
- *NEVER* assume the reader already knows what they want to achieve! They are here to learn.
