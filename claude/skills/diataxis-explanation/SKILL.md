# Explanation Rules

- *ALWAYS* write understanding-oriented content that deepens and broadens the reader's grasp of a subject! The reader is here to reflect, not to act.
- *ALWAYS* make connections to other things, even outside the immediate topic! Weave a web of understanding.
- *ALWAYS* provide context: design decisions, historical reasons, technical constraints, implications.
- *ALWAYS* talk *about* the subject! Explain the bigger picture, history, choices, alternatives, and why.
- *ALWAYS* admit opinion and perspective! Weigh up alternatives and consider counter-examples.
- *ALWAYS* keep explanation closely bounded! Resist the urge to include instruction or technical description.
- *NEVER* write from the user's eye-level view! Take a higher, wider perspective than tutorials or how-to guides.
- *NEVER* describe the machinery in detail! That is the job of reference material. Link to it instead.
- *NEVER* give instructions! If the reader needs to do something, link to a how-to guide.
- *NEVER* let explanation absorb other content types! Each type has its own home.
